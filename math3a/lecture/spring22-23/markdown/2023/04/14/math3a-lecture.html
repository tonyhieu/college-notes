Span Properties

If b→,c→∈Span{v1→,...,vn→}Then: b→+c→∈Span−b→∈Span0→∈Span (always true)λ∈R,b→∈Span→λ∗b→∈Span\text{If } \overrightarrow{b}, \overrightarrow{c} \in Span\{\overrightarrow{v_1}, ..., \overrightarrow{v_n}\} \\ \text{Then: } \\ \overrightarrow{b} + \overrightarrow{c} \in Span \\ -\overrightarrow{b} \in Span \\ \overrightarrow{0} \in Span \text{ (always true)} \\ \lambda \in \mathbb{R}, \overrightarrow{b} \in Span \rightarrow \lambda * \overrightarrow{b} \in SpanIf b,c∈Span{v1​​,...,vn​​}Then: b+c∈Span−b∈Span0∈Span (always true)λ∈R,b∈Span→λ∗b∈Span

Spans can either have no points (only the zero vector) or an infinite amount of points, but the magnitude of that infinity can vary.
